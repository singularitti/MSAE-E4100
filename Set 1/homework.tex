    \begin{equation}
        M = \begin{bmatrix}
                        1 & \bar{1} & 0\\
                        1 & 0           & 0\\
                        0 & 0           & 1
                  \end{bmatrix}^4 =
                   \begin{bmatrix}
                      \bar{1} & 1 & 0\\
                      \bar{1} & 0 & 0\\
                      0 &           0 & 1
              \end{bmatrix}.
    \end{equation}
    Afterthat, by reflected in a plane perpendicular to the $\bm{a}$-axis, the corresponding matrix is 
    $R = \left[\begin{smallmatrix}
        \bar{1} & 0 & 0\\
        0           & 1 & 0\\
        0           & 0 & 1
    \end{smallmatrix}\right]$,
    thus the whole matrix will be 
    \begin{equation}
        M' = M R =\begin{bmatrix}
                             \bar{1} & 1 & 0\\
                             \bar{1} & 0 & 0\\
                             0 &           0 & 1
                        \end{bmatrix}
                        \begin{bmatrix}
                                \bar{1} & 0 & 0\\
                                0           & 1 & 0\\
                                0           & 0 & 1
                        \end{bmatrix} = 
                        \begin{bmatrix}
                                1 & 1 & 0\\
                                1 & 0 & 0\\
                                0 & 0 & 1
                        \end{bmatrix}.
    \end{equation}
    So a general point $(x, y, z)$ will be sent to $(x+y, x, z)$.
